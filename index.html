Solar Panels

What are solar panels?

A solar panel is a device that is composed of solar cells and which, when struck by photons of light from the sun, generates an electrical current which can then be used to power things.

Examples

There are 4 major types of solar panels available on the market today: monocrystalline, polycrystalline, PERC, and thin-film panels

Wind Turbines

What are wind turbines?

Wind turbines use wind to make electricity. Wind turns the propeller-like blades of a turbine around a rotor, which spins a generator, which creates electricity.

Examples

The majority of wind turbines fall into two basic types. Horizontal-Axis turbines and Vertical-Axis turbines

Hydro-Electricity

What is Hydro-Electricity?

Hydro-electric power, is a renewable source of energy that generates power by using things like a dam to alter the natural flow of a river or other body of water. Water flows through a pipe known as a penstock, and then spins the blades in a turbine. Which spins a generator that ultimately produces electricity.

Examples

Environmental Timeline

Date Milestone
April 22, 1970 First Earth Day organized, marking the birth of the modern environmental movement.
December 2, 1970 Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) established in the United States to address environmental issues and regulations.
June 5, 1992 Rio Earth Summit held, leading to the creation of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) to address global climate issues.
December 12, 2015 Paris Agreement adopted, bringing nations together to combat climate change and limit global temperature rise.
